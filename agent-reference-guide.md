# Exploring Alignment Agent: Reference Guide

## Alignment Categories Breakdown
Each alignment category below lists the name, definition, “check alignment” logic, and audience considerations.  
Student Success Drivers also include a **Supports** section that shows which Critical Student Success Goals each driver supports.

---

## Critical Student Success Goals

### 1. Retention
**Definition:** Helping students persist through their first year.  
**Check Alignment:**  
- Improves early engagement, onboarding, or support systems.  
- Enhances first-course teaching.  
- Reduces barriers to entry or confusion.  
**Audience Considerations:**  
- Associate faculty-facing work is often aligned.  
- Full-time faculty work may align if it affects early curriculum or mentoring.  
- Staff-facing work aligns if it supports onboarding or advising.

### 2. Completion
**Definition:** Helping students finish their degrees or certificates.  
**Check Alignment:**  
- Improves instructional quality or consistency.  
- Focuses on assessment, feedback, or scaffolding.  
- Supports long-term academic planning or persistence.  
**Audience Considerations:**  
- Associate faculty work aligns if it improves teaching practices.  
- Full-time faculty work aligns if it affects curriculum design or program structure.  
- Staff work aligns if it supports advising or academic policy.

### 3. Value
**Definition:** Helping students earn credentials with marketable skills.  
**Check Alignment:**  
- Connects instruction to career-relevant outcomes.  
- Emphasizes real-world application.  
- Supports skill-building or employer alignment.  
**Audience Considerations:**  
- Full-time faculty work aligns if it involves curriculum or program design.  
- Associate faculty work aligns if it includes teaching marketable skills.  
- Staff work aligns if it supports career services or employer partnerships.

## UAGC Three-Year Strategic Objectives

1. **Stabilize enrollment through sustainable growth**  
   - Improves student experience or retention.  
   - Supports consistent teaching quality.  
   - Reduces friction in the student journey.  

2. **Evolve academic portfolio and structure**  
   - Curriculum innovation or redesign.  
   - Encourages interdisciplinary or stackable credentials.  
   - Uses AI or technology tools that support new program formats.  

3. **Expand partnerships for workforce alignment**  
   - Connects instruction to employer needs.  
   - Includes career relevance in faculty development.  
   - Supports alumni or employer engagement.  

4. **Exceed peer benchmarks in student success**  
   - Improves retention, completion, or value.  
   - Promotes instructional excellence.  
   - Enhances measurable student outcomes.
